Philips built world-class air purifiers for India. Indian homes still buy them like winter accessories.

This interactive case narrative explains why a fast-growing market, strong technology, and trusted brand still fail to create year-round residential adoption.

The Core Problem

India’s air pollution is chronic and year-round — but consumer demand for air purifiers is seasonal, regional, and reactive.

Why Demand Remains Seasonal

The issue is not affordability alone — it is perception, awareness, and geography.

Myth-Driven Consumption

Consumers believe indoor air is cleaner, AC rooms don’t need purifiers, and pollution is a winter problem.

Geographic Concentration

Sales cluster in North India (Delhi, NCR, Chandigarh) where smog makes headlines.

Reactive Triggers

Purchase happens only when AQI spikes or respiratory symptoms become severe.

Why Philips *Should* Win This Market

On paper, Philips is structurally advantaged.

Technology Credibility

99.97% PM2.5 removal, virus filtration, smart sensing, app control.

Health Brand Trust

Strong repositioning from electronics to health technology.

Distribution Reach

Nationwide retail + e-commerce + service presence in 200+ cities.
Philips does not lack product quality or brand legitimacy — it lacks a dominant *mental category* in the consumer’s mind.

The Strategic Shift Required

Philips must move air purifiers from a pollution response device to preventive health infrastructure — similar to RO purifiers or refrigerators.
