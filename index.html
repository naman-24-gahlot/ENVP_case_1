Philips India launched world-class air purifiers — but Indian homes still buy them like winter jackets.

This case explores why a strong product, in a fast-growing market, struggles to become a year-round necessity in residential India.

The Real Problem (Not the Obvious One)

Pollution in India is not seasonal — but air purifier demand is. The mismatch is not technological. It is perceptual.

The Market Is Exploding

Philips Is Not Weak

Financially resilient. Technologically strong. Strategically constrained.

Philips India Financial Performance

Revenue grows, but profit declines — signalling competitive pressure and limited pricing power in a crowded market.

Personal Health Segment Momentum

Health-led products outperform — validating Philips’ repositioning from electronics to health technology.

Competitive Noise, Not Capability Gap

Philips sits between budget and premium — but consumers don’t clearly understand *why* it should be chosen year-round.

Price vs Performance (CADR)

Philips offers strong performance at mid-range pricing, but lacks sharp mental differentiation.

The Strategic Tension

The challenge is not educating consumers about pollution. It is reframing air purifiers from *reactive devices* to *preventive health infrastructure* inside the home.
